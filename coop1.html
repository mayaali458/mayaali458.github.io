Paules Wetside Boiler Services

As a small drinking water systems assistant at PWBS, I gained valuable hands-on experience in the operation and maintenance of water treatment systems.

My primary responsibilities included:

Collecting water samples from various sampling points at small water systems according to a regular schedule. I performed chemical and microbiological tests on these samples in an accredited laboratory.

Collaborated with health inspectors and engineers to assess conditions and identify improvement opportunities, mastering diligent documentation practices while navigating on-site complexities.

Maintaining accurate daily system logs to track flows, chlorine residuals, turbidity readings, and other parameters. I compiled these into monthly summary reports.

Conducting routine maintenance tasks such as filter backwashing, chlorinator maintenance, and UV lamp replacements to maintain proper operation.

Responding to any unexpected changes in water quality by promptly collecting special samples for analysis.

Through professional collaborations and oversight of multifaceted fieldwork, I strengthened critical thinking, communication, and project leadership skills essential for tackling intricate engineering initiatives. My time at PWBS provided an excellent opportunity to learn about real-world drinking water system operations and strengthened my passion for providing safe, sustainable solutions.
